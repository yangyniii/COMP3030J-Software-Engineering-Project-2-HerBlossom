Ten Life Hacks You Might Want to Know

HerBlossom
April 8, 2025 18 Min Read Skills

Life isn’t always easy, especially when you’re trying to build a better future with limited resources. But small changes can make a big difference. Whether you’re balancing work and family, managing a tight budget, or just starting to take control of your own path, these ten life hacks are here to support you. They’re simple, practical, and designed to help you save time, energy, and maybe even a bit of stress. You are strong, capable, and not alone—here’s to making life a little easier, one clever tip at a time.

🏠 Home Safety

1. Upgrade Your Locks & Use Extra Security Measures

For women living alone, the first line of defense is the door. Consider upgrading to a smart lock that supports remote access, and add physical security features like deadbolts, door chains, or anti-peep viewers. Always double-check that the door is locked when leaving or going to bed. Never hide spare keys under mats or in shoes—these are obvious spots for intruders.

2. The “Fake Roommate” Strategy

When delivery people or repair workers come to your door, avoid revealing that you live alone. Say things like “My roommate is sleeping” or “My friend will answer in a minute.” This creates the illusion that you’re not alone and helps deter unwanted attention or threats.

⚠️ Emergency Preparedness

3. Carry a Personal Safety Device

Carrying a small self-defense tool doesn't mean you're paranoid—it just gives you a chance to escape danger if needed. A pepper spray, safety whistle, mini stun gun, or alarm keychain can be life-saving in emergencies. Keep it somewhere easily accessible, like your coat pocket or purse, and make sure you know how to use it in advance.

4. Set a “Safety Contact”

Before heading out alone, riding in a stranger's car, or going to unfamiliar places, share your real-time location with a trusted friend or family member. Set up emergency contacts in your phone—iPhone users can do this via the “Health” app, while Android users can find it under emergency settings. In a crisis, you can alert someone with just one tap.

🧰 Practical Living Skills

5. Learn Basic Home Repair Skills

Living alone means you’ll need to handle things like clogged toilets, power outages, or leaking faucets on your own. Learn basic maintenance skills like how to reset a circuit breaker, unclog drains, or replace light bulbs. There are plenty of beginner-friendly videos online. Being prepared helps you stay calm and independent during minor home emergencies.

6. Prepare a Small Toolkit

Every woman living alone should have her own mini toolbox. Include a screwdriver set (flathead and Phillips), tape, pliers, a small hammer, electric tape, and a measuring tape. These tools come in handy for hanging decorations, fixing furniture, or doing small repairs without needing to call for help.

🍽️ Nutrition & Food Prep

7. Learn 3–5 Quick, Healthy Meals

Cooking doesn’t need to be complicated. Master a few easy, nutritious recipes like tomato and egg stir-fry, sautéed greens, pan-fried chicken breast, or oven-baked rice dishes. These meals are faster, healthier, and cheaper than takeout. Even just a little cooking skill can significantly improve your daily life and long-term health.

8. Keep Emergency Food and Water at Home

Always store some non-perishable items at home like instant porridge, canned food, dried fruits, nuts, and bottled water. These are essential in situations like bad weather, power outages, or days when you're feeling unwell and can’t go out. Having a small food reserve ensures you feel safe and prepared.

🧘‍♀️ Mental & Emotional Health

9. Practice Regular “Emotional Detox”

Living alone can sometimes lead to feelings of loneliness or anxiety. Find ways to release emotional buildup—journaling, meditation, yoga, hot baths, or talking to a friend all help. You can also join online communities or support groups for women living solo. Don’t bottle up your feelings—emotional health is just as important as physical well-being.

10. Build Daily Micro-Habits for Self-Discipline

A stable routine gives you a sense of control and peace. Set small, manageable goals like waking up at the same time, drinking more water, reading 10 minutes a day, or stretching before bed. These micro-habits may seem small but they bring structure to your life and reduce stress over time.
